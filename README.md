##Capstone Project Title:
#TRACKING DIGITAL INDIA- 
Analytics and Forecasting of AI Adoption Among Indian Students.

##Problem Statement:
Currently, Artificial Intelligence tools are rapidly being introduced into the educational ecosystem,
fundamentally changing how Indian students across various streams approach tasks like assignments,
doubt solving, and coding help. It is important to accurately track and understand this digital
transition to ensure equitable access and to gauge the current awareness and trust levels among the
student population. Eventually, addressing the clear disparities in digital infrastructure—specifically
the high percentages of students experiencing poor (32.65%) or medium (35.65%) internet
connectivity —alongside the friction of academic acceptance from professors, becomes a major
concern for educational institutions. The crucial part is the comprehensive analysis and forecasting of
these AI adoption trends to effectively bridge the digital divide and support the broader goals of
Tracking Digital India.​

##Tools Used: Power BI, Power Query, DAX, Kaggle, MS Learn, Fice, Github, etc.

##Key Insights:
#Awareness vs. Trust- Out of the 2000 total students surveyed , the average AI awareness level is moderate at 5.73 out of 10. However, the average trust in AI remains low at 2.99.

#Top Tool Preferences- The most preferred AI tools are highly competitive, led by ChatGPT with 433 users , followed closely by Copilot with 432 users and Gemini with 424 users.

#Usage Habits: Students spend an average of 2.64 hours daily on these tools , primarily for use cases involving assignments and coding help.

#Device Accessibility: Device usage is remarkably evenly distributed, with 34.3% using Laptops , 33.5% using Tablets , and 32.2% relying on Mobile devices.

#Commercial Potential vs. Academic Friction: While a majority of 51.3% of students are willing to pay for AI tools , there is a significant institutional barrier, as 50.95% of professors do not allow AI usage.

#Infrastructure Bottlenecks: A major hurdle for digital adoption is connectivity; only 31.7% of students have high internet access , while the rest struggle with medium (35.65%) or poor (32.65%) access.
