📊 TRACKING DIGITAL INDIA: AI Adoption Among Indian Students – Power BI


🧠 The Story Behind This Project:

Currently, Artificial Intelligence tools are rapidly being introduced into the educational ecosystem, fundamentally changing how Indian students across various streams approach tasks like assignments, doubt solving, and coding help.
Instead of just building charts on tool usage, I wanted to explore a real-world socio-economic question:
How are Indian students adapting to the AI revolution, and what barriers stand in their way?
It is important to accurately track and understand this digital transition to ensure equitable access and to gauge the current awareness and trust levels among the student population. Addressing clear disparities in digital infrastructure alongside the friction of academic acceptance from professors has become a major concern for educational institutions. Through this project, I tried to comprehensively analyze and forecast these AI adoption trends to effectively bridge the digital divide and support the broader goals of Tracking Digital India.

🔍 What This Dashboard Explores:


The dashboard focuses on analyzing student survey data to identify patterns behind AI adoption, trust levels, and infrastructure bottlenecks.
Some of the questions explored in this project include:
What are the current awareness and trust levels of students regarding AI?
Which AI tools are the most preferred in the educational space?
How much time do students spend daily on these tools, and for what primary use cases?
What devices are students primarily using to access these technologies?
How does the students' willingness to pay for AI contrast with institutional restrictions?
How severely does poor internet connectivity impact digital adoption?


📈 Key Insights from the Data:

After analyzing the dataset of 2000 surveyed students, several interesting patterns emerged:
Awareness vs. Trust: The average AI awareness level is moderate at 5.73 out of 10, however, the average trust in AI remains critically low at 2.99.
Top Tool Preferences: The landscape is highly competitive, led by ChatGPT (433 users), followed closely by Copilot (432 users) and Gemini (424 users).
Usage Habits: Students spend an average of 2.64 hours daily on these tools, primarily for use cases involving assignments and coding help.
Device Accessibility: Device usage is remarkably evenly distributed, with 34.3% using Laptops, 33.5% using Tablets, and 32.2% relying on Mobile devices.
Commercial Potential vs. Academic Friction: While a majority of 51.3% of students are willing to pay for AI tools, there is a significant institutional barrier, as 50.95% of professors do not allow AI usage.
Infrastructure Bottlenecks: Connectivity is a major hurdle. Only 31.7% of students have high-speed internet access, while the rest struggle with medium (35.65%) or poor (32.65%) access.
